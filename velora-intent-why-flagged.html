The case

Dr. Anjali Pandey · GenMed · Wednesday morning

Dr. Pandey runs an active panel of 142 patients at the General Medicine OPD. Her morning starts at 07:50 with a coffee and a glance at the day's schedule — 18 OPD slots, 4 admissions to round on. 23 of her 142 patients are flagged this morning for one of three care-gap queries the hospital signed off on. None of those 23 are on today's schedule. They are the patients she is not about to see.

Population queries, not patient drawers. The gap surface answers a different question from the chart: not "what's on this patient" but "who am I missing?" Three pre-validated queries, signed off by the GenMed lead before going live, run overnight against the hospital's complete data. By 07:48, the patients are listed.

"I was about to start clinic. The 12 diabetics with no HbA1c in 6 months took ten seconds to read. Two of them were due back this month anyway — I just told my staff to add an HbA1c order to their next visit. That's a Velora insight acted on, before I sat down." — Dr. Pandey · pilot week 3 · 8 May 2026
How the gap surface looks

23 patients flagged this morning. Three queries. One panel.

Care gaps is a population surface, not a patient drawer. The doctor opens it once a day and sees a list. Each gap query is a deterministic Cypher signed off by the clinical lead before pilot launch — no LLM judgment in the row, only in the optional ranking. Click any patient to open their MDT brief; the trace at the bottom shows the query that produced this list.

Same gap · different hospital

Same gap. Different hospital. Different threshold wins.

"Diabetic with no HbA1c in 6 months" is one hospital's gap. Another hospital uses 4 months because its diabetic-care protocol is tighter. "Discharge with no follow-up in 30 days" is operational config — different hospitals choose different windows. Velora's job is to run this hospital's signed gap thresholds and cite which rule fired.
