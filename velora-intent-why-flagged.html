A patient on today's OPD list isn't just an appointment. They are an admission you might have missed, a critical lab that never made it back to a note, a vital reading drifting outside the threshold someone signed off on.

The schedule sees them as a 09:20 slot. Velora sees the four structured events that fired since the last clinical contact — and surfaces them, cited, before the patient walks in.

It is a librarian, not a triage system. It surfaces structured signals; it does not prioritise.

What you're watching

Stack 1 is the four structured events, each tagged with severity (critical / warning / neutral) drawn from the rule that fired — never from an LLM classifier.

Stack 2 names the rule for each event and cites its source: the hospital's lab reference range (signed by Pathology lead), WHO HEARTS for BP, NICE NG56 for polypharmacy, the hospital's operational admission window for the post-discharge marker.

Defence: severity tiers are structured-rule output. The LLM may not classify a flag as critical. Velora surfaces a fact and the rule it breached; the doctor decides the action.
The product

The schedule shows the slot. Velora shows the events that fired.

Velora runs five detectors per patient on the morning panel — admission in window, unreviewed critical lab, threshold breach on a recorded vital, polypharmacy count, missed follow-up. Each detector is a deterministic Cypher; each fires only against a structured event with a stable id. Velora's surface renders the union, severity-sorted, with the rule that fired cited per row.

How the drawer is structured

Two stacks. Two epistemic levels. Visually separated.

For Why-flagged-today, Stack 1 is the structured event itself — admission, lab, vital, count. Stack 2 is the rule that fired — the threshold, the window, the count criterion — each cited to a guideline body or a signed hospital config. Severity tiers are computed from rules, never from a classifier.

Stack 1 Hospital records Fact

Structured events with severity tier from rules. No AI authoring.

Click any flag to open the source admission, lab, vital, or chart row in the hospital's records.

  • Admission events in the configured window (default 30 days)
  • Critical-flagged labs unreviewed (no subsequent note)
  • Vital readings exceeding signed thresholds
  • Polypharmacy count exceeding NICE NG56 trigger
  • Missed follow-ups in the configured window
Defence: every flag traces to a structured event id (admission_id, result_id, vital_id, appointment_id). Click → source. Reproducible.
Stack 2 AI Interpretation · informational only Suggested

Threshold rule cited per flag. Severity is rule-based, never LLM-classified.

Each rule is either a guideline body section or a signed hospital config row. The card names which.

  • Lab thresholds — hospital lab reference range, signed by Pathology lead
  • BP thresholds — WHO HEARTS · ICMR HTN · AHA (per hospital config)
  • Polypharmacy trigger — NICE NG56 · ADA · ICMR (per hospital config)
  • Admission window — hospital operational config (signed)
  • Follow-up window — hospital operational config (signed)
Defence: Velora does not call a flag critical. The lab's signed reference range does. Velora only renders the comparison and cites the rule.

Stack 1 is always above Stack 2. A doctor with 5 seconds reads only Stack 1. A doctor with 15 seconds reads both.

Same event · different hospital

Same event. Different hospital. Different threshold wins.

A K+ of 5.9 might be tier-warning at one hospital and tier-critical at another, depending on the lab's signed reference range. An admission in the last 30 days might or might not flag depending on the hospital's operational window. Velora's job is to apply this hospital's signed thresholds and cite which rule fired.
