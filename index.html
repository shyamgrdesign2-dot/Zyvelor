The story · a busy multi-specialty hospital

By 9am, a doctor has met three kinds of patient.

Each one needs a different first move from Velora. The patient never sees these labels — Velora detects the situation from patient × this_doctor × hospital and adapts the surface accordingly.

1

Already in your care

Patient you've seen before · your own notes on file
Your memory and prior charts cover most. Velora fills the in-between — what other specialties did between your visits, and which loops you started haven't closed.
Augments memory
2

New to your care, known here

First visit with you · the hospital has history
You walk in cold to a patient the hospital already knows. Cardio started a drug last week, Endo flagged a lab, Pulmo has an open consult. Velora stitches the cross-specialty picture before the consult.
Cross-specialty stitching
3

First visit to the hospital

Brand new to the hospital · or referred in with paper records
No prior records → honest empty state. If they walk in with external documents (referral letter, scanned labs, prior Rx, imaging), Velora OCRs and structures them into Stack 1, tagged external · unverified.

Clinical Guidelines

Every clinical claim cites a published body. Hospitals choose which body wins.

Velora catalogues the recognised guideline bodies for every specialty — global, regional, and India-specific — and tags each one with the conditions it covers. The hospital's deployment config picks which body wins per condition. It is not a fixed list — hospitals can use one body, mix several, or swap mid-pilot. Velora's engine doesn't change; only the cited authority does.

The full catalogue — every body per specialty, with the publishing organisation and source URL — lives on its own page.
