The four intents · what Velora does for a doctor

One platform. Four jobs every multi-specialty doctor has, every day.

MDT is the anchor — the cross-specialty deep-dive when a patient walks in. The other three orbit it, each with its own UI shape: Care Gaps as a population list, Medication Red Flags as a prescribe-time checkpoint, Visit recap as a return-visit timeline. Same engine. Same citations. Different shape per intent — the MDT format isn't force-fit on any of them.

Clinically defensible in Indian OPD

Every flag in V0 is anchored to an event that already exists in the hospital's records — an admission, a referral that was sent, a critical lab that was drawn, a prescription that was written. No "find the patients you might be missing" framing — those don't fit Indian OPD without recall infrastructure. The four intents below all act on patients who are already in the system.

01 · Anchor intent

MDT brief — read across the silos when you open a patient

Cross-specialty stitched dossier. What each team has said, what's pending across them, the patient's journey across visits, with each clinical claim cited to the guideline body the hospital chose. Stack 1 facts. Stack 2 guideline-anchored panels. The deep view, before consultation.

Open the MDT walkthrough
02 · Population

Care Gaps — expected next step is missing

Of the patients you've touched, who has an action that should have happened by now and didn't. Discharged 22 d ago · no follow-up booked. HbA1c returned high · no clinical note attached. Referral sent · no destination visit. Each gap anchored to a structured event that already happened.

Open
03 · Action checkpoint

Medication Red Flags — DDI · allergy · target

At the prescribe click: drug-drug interaction, allergy match, drug-target mismatch (HbA1c > 8 on metformin, BP uncontrolled on a BP drug), polypharmacy. Each card cites a Lexicomp / formulary / ICMR rule. The doctor accepts or overrides; nothing is auto-stopped.

Open
04 · Returning patient

Visit recap — what happened last time, what's still pending

For a patient walking back in: their last visit's plan, which orders came back, which referrals landed, what's changed since. The 30-second pre-read before the door opens on a follow-up.

Clinical Guidelines

Every clinical claim cites a published body. Hospitals choose which body wins.

Velora catalogues the recognised guideline bodies for every specialty — global, regional, and India-specific — and tags each one with the conditions it covers. The hospital's deployment config picks which body wins per condition. It is not a fixed list — hospitals can use one body, mix several, or swap mid-pilot. Velora's engine doesn't change; only the cited authority does.

The full catalogue — every body per specialty, with the publishing organisation and source URL — lives on its own page.
