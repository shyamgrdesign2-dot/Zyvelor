Clinical Guidelines

Every clinical claim cites a body. The hospital chooses which.

Velora catalogues the recognised guideline bodies for every specialty — global, regional, and India-specific. The hospital's deployment config picks which body wins per condition. Use one, mix several, swap mid-pilot. The engine doesn't change; the cited authority does.

How configurability works. Hospitals don't have to follow every guideline listed below. They follow the ones their clinical advisory board has signed off on.

One hospital might use only ESC 2024 for AF and AHA/ACC 2022 for HF. Another might mix bodies — ICMR for diabetes, ADA for lipids, KDIGO for CKD staging. A smaller centre might run a single set across the board. Velora reads the signed config; cards cite the body that won.

How a hospital configures the library

One file. Signed off. Read by Velora at every claim.

The hospital's hospital.yaml binds each domain.subdomain to one or more guideline bodies. Velora's engine reads the chosen body, applies its rule, and cites the section on the answer card.

Sample · medanta_hospital.yaml

What 12 lines of config buy you

A nephrologist at Medanta sees Ravi Shankar's PD adequacy card cite ISN-India 2020 §4.2. The same patient at Mayo would see the same card cite KDIGO 2006 §weekly Kt/V. Same engine, same patient, different body — because the config said so.

# medanta_hospital.yaml — clinical advisory board signed 2026-04-15

# Cardiology
cardiology.atrial_fibrillation: ESC 2024

How this list is curated

How we chose what's in the library — and the proof per body.

The list above isn't speculative. Every body included meets at least one of three criteria. Hover any body's card to see its publishing organisation and its source URL — that is the proof.

01

Adoption signal

Endorsed by a major clinical society, taught at residency level, cited in MoHFW programmes, or referenced in standard textbooks at Indian medical schools.

02

Active maintenance

Updated within the last 5 years, with a public update cadence. Bodies that are dormant or merged into successor organisations are excluded or flagged.

03

India relevance

Either a global body widely used in India, an India-specific society (FOGSI, IAP, ICMR, ISN-India, etc.), or a national programme (NTEP, MoHFW maternal health).

04

Public source

Each body must have a publicly accessible homepage and a published location for its latest guidance. Closed-only or paywalled-only bodies aren't included unless the abstract layer (rule + section) is public.

If a body is missing. The list is not exhaustive. If a body matters at your hospital and isn't here, the rule library accepts it as long as it meets the four criteria above. New bodies are added once and immediately available to every hospital via config — without a code change.
