The case

Lakshmi Pandey returns · 10:20 OPD · last seen 12 March

Lakshmi was last in this clinic six weeks ago. The note from that visit set a plan — five things were started: an HbA1c, a referral to Cardiology for atypical chest pain, a fasting glucose, atorvastatin 20 mg, and a follow-up booked for 10 March. She didn't make the follow-up. None of the five was fully closed. When Dr. Pandey opens the chart at 10:18, the EMR shows the last note. Velora goes one step further: it shows what came of each plan item, and what other departments did with her in the gap.

Same recap · different hospital

Same plan items. Different hospital. Different staleness thresholds.

There is no clinical guideline that says "a fasting glucose is stale at 7 days." That is a workflow choice the hospital makes — based on OPD volume, lab turnaround, referral patterns. Two hospitals can each be correct and pick different numbers.

Velora's job is to apply this hospital's signed-off config and label each plan item against it. Same code at Medanta and Mayo. Different windows.
